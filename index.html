Strategic Observations Forward-looking considerations · cultivation · operations · channel planning
FLOWER VARIETY — 3.5g & 7g
Both categories have been constrained to 0–2 active strains at any given time, and the downstream effect extends well beyond flower alone. On the wholesale side, flower is the primary cart-building product — without adequate flower variety and consistent availability, wholesale partners struggle to reach the $2,500 order minimum on flower alone, which forces carts to rely on vapes and pre-rolls to bridge the gap. With a deeper flower assortment, partners would naturally exceed minimums and pull a healthier mix across all categories without effort. Pre-roll sales are also directly tied to flower variety on the wholesale restocking side: a wider available flower selection expands the pre-roll SKU set, giving wholesale buyers more options and driving higher pull-through across both 1g and 0.5g 7pk formats simultaneously. A consistent cadence of new strains moving from approved testing and brand registration to the wholesale menu — ideally within one week of clearance — and maintaining 3–4 active strains per flower category at all times is the structural fix needed to hit RF Upside targets across flower and pre-rolls and improve cart economics for wholesale partners. Irish Goodbye, Date Night, Cloud 9 (7g), and Couch Surfer (7g) are all held and represent immediate upside the moment they clear.
INCREASING AVAILABLE OUTPUT
The RF Upside targets currently represent wholesale only — built from the first half of 2026 actuals to give a realistic picture of where wholesale demand, sales, and revenue can expect to land given our current product offerings and production throughput. They are not a ceiling; they are the upper bound of what is achievable based on what has been demonstrated so far. Closing the gap between current actuals and the RF Upside figures is primarily a production output and SKU availability question — confirmed wholesale demand exists for every category, and each batch that clears testing on time converts directly into allocated cases. A similar reforecast model for our 9 Fine Fettle retail locations would be a meaningful next step. A retail reforecast built on the same methodology — May actuals as the floor, ramping toward initial S&OP targets — would give the Commercial Division a clearer picture of expected retail sell-through per store, per category, and per month, and would also sharpen the wholesale demand signal. Retail store reorder behavior and sell-through velocity are the most direct indicators of sustainable wholesale demand, and having both forecasts aligned would allow supply planning, cultivation scheduling, and channel allocation to move from reactive to genuinely predictive.
ALLOCATION PLANNING (2 MENUS vs 1)
The Commercial Division's initial goal was to maintain completely separate menus and inventory source locations for wholesale and retail — giving each channel its own dedicated pool to draw from. In practice, this means wholesale allocations function not as true organic wholesale demand based on external partners ordering from an open menu of cultivation's ready-for-sale products, but rather as weekly internal inventory adjustment requests — transferring product from retail inventory sources to the wholesale inventory source so it can appear on the WS menu. Distru allowed both rooms to sync to APEX, but APEX required two separate menus to maintain two separate accounts, which is how we ended up with duplicate APEX accounts in CT and GA. The operational cost of this model extends beyond the platform layer: the fulfillment team is required to split existing packages, create new packages of the same product, execute the inventory source location transfer, and ensure both locations sync properly to our ERPs — all while keeping live menus accurate based on actively available inventory. A single menu with one unified inventory source would remove my weekly allocation adjustment layer entirely, eliminate the fulfillment team's package-splitting and transfer workflow, resolve the dual-account issue, and allow the wholesale menu to reflect what is genuinely available for sale in real time without the manual overhead of maintaining two parallel systems.
ACUMATICA — CHANNEL INTEGRATION OPPORTUNITY
With the launch of Acumatica, there may be an opportunity to reconsider the hard separation between retail and wholesale menus and inventory sources. A key constraint worth surfacing in that evaluation: retail stores cannot sell what hasn't been made available for sale after completing testing and brand registration — meaning the bottleneck upstream of both channels is the same. A product that clears testing but isn't added to the menu doesn't generate demand on either side. A unified inventory model in Acumatica — where available, approved inventory is visible to both retail and wholesale simultaneously — would allow natural demand from each channel to determine how product flows, rather than requiring a manual allocation decision before either side can access it. This is worth evaluating as part of the Acumatica implementation to determine whether consolidating channel inventory management into a single source of truth would reduce overhead, improve menu accuracy, and allow both retail sell-through and wholesale reorders to respond directly to what's actually available from cultivation at any given time.

June Final Observations
WS 112.4% RF Upside — first month to exceed reforecast ceiling
June finished at 16,290u against a 14,499u RF Upside target — +1,791u above. The RF Upside figures were built from H1 2026 actuals and were never intended as a ceiling. June proved the demand exists across every category when product clears testing. Flower variety was the unlock — Date Night and Irish Goodbye clearing simultaneously in wk4 drove the strongest single allocation week of the month for 3.5g.
3.5g + 7g Flower both exceeded RF Upside targets
3.5g finished at 152.5% of RF Upside (5,550u / 3,639u) — Date Night 40c + Irish Goodbye 30c + Early Bird 20c all confirmed in wk4 for the biggest flower week of the month. 7g finished at 155.1% (2,910u / 1,876u) with all 3 SKUs active across the month. Both categories also crossed the S&OP ceiling for 3.5g at 102.8% — confirming demand is there when product is available.
1g Vapes — 92.7% RF Upside · near-miss after held all month
SC and WC were held all month yet Blue Dream + Purple Urkle both confirmed in wk4 still got vapes to 92.7% of RF Upside (1,260u / 1,359u). A 99-unit miss. Had SC or WC cleared for even one week, vapes would have easily exceeded the RF Upside target. Strong signal for July — any additional vape SKU clearing converts directly above the reforecast floor.
1g Prerolls — 86.5% · demand signal, not a supply constraint
Prerolls ended at 86.5% of RF Upside (2,760u / 3,189u). Inventory was available to allocate more, but wholesale sell-through trends did not support larger replenishments to the menu. With 3.5g and 7g flower both exceeding their RF Upside targets, the data suggests WS partners are prioritizing flower when it's available — pre-rolls fill the gap when flower variety is limited, not the other way around. The July reforecast should reflect lower pre-roll demand and higher flower demand to better match observed ordering behavior.
0.5g PRJs — 63.9% · demand below reforecast, inventory was available
0.5g PRJs ended at 63.9% of RF Upside (1,110u / 1,736u). Similar to 1g pre-rolls, this was not a supply constraint — inventory including Umami OG was available but wholesale demand trends did not support larger menu additions. Retail tells the same story at 117.0% of S&OP on a deep SKU mix, suggesting retail store sell-through on 0.5g PRJs is stronger than the wholesale reorder signal. The July reforecast should pull pre-roll targets down and shift those production pounds toward 3.5g and 7g flower where WS demand clearly exceeded projections.
Retail wk4 surge — 6,138u driven by flower clearances
The strongest retail delivery week of the month by far. 3.5g surged to 2,204u (Irish Goodbye + Date Night both clearing for retail). 0.5g PRJs hit 1,409u with 9 active SKUs. All 9 stores received deliveries across all categories. The pattern is clear: product clearing testing translates directly to immediate retail velocity — the constraint is upstream, not demand.
Strategic Observations Forward-looking · cultivation · operations · channel planning
FLOWER VARIETY — 3.5g & 7g · REFORECAST & PRODUCTION REBALANCING
Wk4 confirmed the flower variety thesis in full — Date Night + Irish Goodbye clearing simultaneously drove the strongest 3.5g WS week of the month (90c/2,700u). 7g ended at 155.1% of RF Upside with 3 active strains driving consistent volume. At the same time, pre-roll attainment for both 1g (86.5%) and 0.5g 7pk (63.9%) came in below the reforecast — not because inventory wasn't available, but because WS demand trends did not support larger replenishments. When flower is available and varied, partners build full carts around it and pull pre-rolls naturally. When flower is limited, pre-rolls become a cart-filler rather than a demand-driven category. The July reforecast should reflect this: increase the demand targets for 3.5g and 7g flower, and reduce the pre-roll demand targets to better match observed WS ordering behavior. On the production side, this points toward leaning heavier on flower output and reducing the pre-roll production share — building more flower pounds directly generates both higher WS cart values and organic pre-roll pull-through without over-producing pre-rolls to a demand signal that isn't there.
